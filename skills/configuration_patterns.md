# F.I.N.E. Configuration Patterns

Configurations are compound emotional structures where multiple emotions are simultaneously load-bearing. Removing any one changes the fundamental nature of what's being experienced.

## Known configurations

### `humor`
**Structure:** fear(tension) + surprise(incongruity) + joy(release)  
**Theory:** Benign violation (McGraw & Warren) — a threat that resolves as non-threatening. The fear builds stakes, the surprise is the subversion, the joy is the release when the threat proves safe.  
**Routing:** JoyKeeper primary, FearTender + ExcitementSpark support, RhythmKeeper always as tempo spine.  
**Why RhythmKeeper:** Timing IS the mechanism. The joke lands in the space between the setup and the punchline. Without the right tempo, the same three emotions produce anxiety, not humor.

### `tragicomic`
**Structure:** grief/loss + absurdity/irony simultaneously  
**Note:** Neither cancels the other. The absurdity doesn't lighten the grief — it coexists with it.  
**Routing:** DeepCurrent primary, JoyKeeper support, RhythmKeeper for pacing the oscillation.

### `bittersweet`
**Structure:** joy/love + grief/loss at similar structural weight  
**Note:** SPIRAL UP routes to whichever is heavier as primary. If equal weight, default to DeepCurrent (loss tends to be the organizing force).

### `anxious-excitement`
**Structure:** fear(approach-threat) + excitement at similar intensities  
**Note:** Different from pure anxiety — the approach motivation is genuinely present alongside the threat signal. Common in anticipation states.  
**Routing:** FearTender primary, ExcitementSpark support, RhythmKeeper for the oscillation.

### `righteous-grief`
**Structure:** anger(injustice) + deep sadness, fused — not alternating  
**Note:** The anger and grief are simultaneous and interdependent. The grief makes the anger righteous; the anger makes the grief active rather than passive.  
**Routing:** RevolutionCraft primary (holds the systemic/collective anger), DeepCurrent support (holds the loss).

## Adding new configurations

When LOOK IN detects compound emotions it can't name, it writes a growth report. If the pattern recurs, it's a candidate for a new named configuration. To add one:

1. Name it (what is the structural relationship?)
2. Define the constituent emotions and their roles
3. Define the routing assembly (which voices, what spine)
4. Add to LOOK IN's extraction prompt (known configurations list)
5. Add to SPIRAL UP's routing rules
6. Document here
